Why Goals Matter

You're about to invest significant time and energy into this program. Without clear goals, that investment can feel scattered and unmeasurable. With clear goals, every lesson and exercise becomes a step toward a defined destination.

Research consistently shows that people who write down their goals are significantly more likely to achieve them. This exercise will help you define what success looks like for your entrepreneurial journey.

The SMART Goal Framework

Effective goals are SMART:

S

Specific

Clearly defined and detailed. "Grow my business" is vague. "Acquire 10 paying customers" is specific.

M

Measurable

Quantifiable so you know when you've achieved it. Include numbers, dates, or other concrete metrics.

A

Achievable

Challenging but realistic given your resources and constraints. Stretch goals are good; impossible goals are demotivating.

R

Relevant

Aligned with your larger vision and current priorities. Ask: "Does this goal move me toward my bigger objectives?"

T

Time-bound

Has a deadline. For this program, your primary deadline is 7 weeks from now.

Three Levels of Goals

For maximum clarity, set goals at three different levels:

Summit Goal (End of Program)

What does success look like at the end of Week 7? This is your primary destination.

Camp Goals (Weekly Milestones)

What should you accomplish each week to stay on track? These are your checkpoints.

Trail Goals (Daily/Lesson Actions)

What specific actions will you take in each lesson or each day? These keep you moving.
